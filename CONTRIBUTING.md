# Contributing

Thank you for helping improve Tech CV Guide.

## Useful contributions

Good contributions include:

- correcting inaccurate or outdated guidance;
- replacing vague advice with a clearer explanation;
- adding realistic before-and-after examples;
- adding role-specific examples for software, support, security, data, or cloud;
- improving accessibility and reading order;
- fixing broken links, spelling, or formatting;
- citing a reliable source for a factual recommendation.

## Content principles

Contributions should be:

- practical and easy to understand;
- honest about uncertainty and regional differences;
- useful to students and early-career professionals;
- based on evidence rather than recruiter folklore;
- free of guaranteed-outcome claims;
- written in original language, without copying protected templates or articles.

## Before opening a pull request

1. Check existing issues and pull requests.
2. Keep one contribution focused on one topic.
3. Explain what changed and why.
4. Test every internal and external link.
5. Preview the Markdown on GitHub.
6. Remove real personal data from examples.
7. Cite the source when introducing a factual claim.

## Style

- Use short paragraphs and descriptive headings.
- Prefer plain English over unnecessary jargon.
- Show examples when a rule may be misunderstood.
- Use **Weak**, **Better**, and **Why** comparisons when useful.
- Do not claim that one layout, phrase, or keyword strategy guarantees ATS success.
- Use accessible link labels instead of “click here.”

## Privacy

Do not submit:

- real phone numbers or private email addresses;
- home addresses or identity numbers;
- API keys, passwords, tokens, or private repository links;
- employer-confidential information;
- a person's CV without their clear permission.

By contributing, you confirm that you have the right to submit the content.
